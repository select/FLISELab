Zoom:

Mouse over to highlight individual values. The coordinates are: the calculated time in seconds, with the raw recorded time in parenthesis; each series name and value (with customed defined colors and visibility as defined in the "series options" panel).

Click and drag to zoom in (vertical and horizontal). Double-click to come back to the original scale.

You can also use the mousewheel to zoom along the time axis. Alt-click (or shift-click) and drag can be used to move along the time axis (works with any tool)
Cut: Click to create a separation between two portions of the time series. Each defined segment is an experiment and can thus be exported with the segment export tool. It will also split a no-cut interval (priority).
No differentiate: Click and drag to define a timed interval when differentiation will be skipped.
Drop: Click and drag to define a timed interval when the data can be discarded (no segment export). The auto-segmentation method (panel "Auto-segmentation") will place automatically "drop"-segments (the window size is used to compute the local statistics; the distance to fuse indicates the minimal length an interval can have to be considered as an experiment, otherwise it is fused with the neighbouring "drop"-segments). "Drop"-segments have priority over cuts and "no-cut"-segments.
Event:

Events can be either attached to series (click on the series at the desired time) or to all the series (click away from the series at the desired time). A window will popup to further define the event. Mouse over to know the time of event defined (text); you can edit an event by clicking at the exact same time (at the flag marker) for the same series (or away if it concerns all series).

Series event types: comment; calibration (for the series measured component).

All series event types: comment; wash (precise the initial solution and the inital volume); injection (precise what solution is added, for which volume and concentration); dilution (precise the amount of pure water added); removal (precise the volume of medium removed).

NOTE: any experiment segment should be always preceded by a wash-event to set the initial volume (otherwise nul).
Cancel: Click and drag to defined an interval within which any cut, event, fully covered no-cut and drop segments are removed.

(Hint: any tool action can be immediatly reverted with the "Undo" button.)
